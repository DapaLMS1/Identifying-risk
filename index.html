Step 1: Find the Hazards
Start by looking around your dental practice. Check all the treatment rooms, the lab, the waiting area, and any other spaces.
Step 2: Think About the Harm
Consider what kind of injury or illness someone could get from each hazard. Could they slip and fall? Could they get an infection?
Step 3: Think About How It Could Happen
Imagine the different ways someone could get hurt. For example, how could someone trip over a cord?
Step 4: Think About How Likely It Is
Decide how often you think the harm could happen. Is it something that happens a lot, or is it rare?
Step 5: Decide How to Fix It
Think about what you can do to stop the harm. Can you move the cord? Can you clean up the spill?
Step 6: Write It Down
Keep a record of your risk assessment. Write down what you found and what you plan to do about it.
Step 7: Check Again Later
Review your risk assessment regularly. Things can change in the dental practice, so you need to keep checking for new hazards.
